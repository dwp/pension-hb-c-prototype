Apply for Pension Credit and Housing Benefit

If you are of state pension age, you can use this service to apply for both Pension Credit and Housing Benefit at the same time.

Eligibility

You should only use this service if you believe you are eligible for both. If you are only eligible for one of the benefits, you should apply separately.

You can use this service to check if you are eligible before you apply.

Applying for Pension Credit

You can apply for Pension Credit if you:

  • have a low income
  • are resident in the UK
  • are part of a mixed age couple, where one of you is not of pension age
  • have appropriate immigration status
  • you have deferred your pension

You should not apply for Pension Credit if you:

  • are currently away from Great Britain for more than 4 weeks, unless it is for medical treatment or recovery
  • are already outside Great Britain
  • are moving away from Great Britain permanently

Applying for Housing Benefit

You can apply for Housing Benefit if you:

  • have a low income
  • are resident in the UK
  • have a total of less than £16,000 in savings, investments, or other assets
  • either rent the home that you live in, or a private room in it

You will be automatically entitled to claim Housing Benefit if you are claiming any of the following:

  • Income Support
  • Income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • Income related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Guarantee Pension Credit / the Guarantee Credit element of Pension Credit

You should not apply for Housing Benefit if you:

  • own your own home
  • live in temporary or supported accommodation
  • live with someone related to you, and they are responsible for the rent
  • are part of a mixed age couple, where one of you is not of pension age

Information you will need

You will need to tell us about:

  • your immigration status, and whether you have spent time outside of the UK
  • your rental situation, including landlord or agency details
  • information about any other people living with you
  • the dates you have lived in different addresses
  • your income, money and investments
  • any assets, including land or property you own
