Overview

Pension Credit gives you extra money to help with your living costs if you are over State Pension age and on a low income. You can apply for Housing Benefit at the same time.

{% from "govuk/components/inset-text/macro.njk" import govukInsetText %} {{ govukInsetText({ text: "This guide is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg) and easy read format." }) }}

You might get extra help if you are a carer, severely disabled, or responsible for a child or young person.

Pension Credit and Housing Benefit are separate from your State Pension.

You may be able to get Pension Credit and Housing Benefit even if you have other income, savings or own your own home.

Other help if you get Pension Credit

If you get Pension Credit you can also get other help, such as:

  • Cold Weather Payments (or Winter Heating Payments in Scotland)
  • Support for Mortgage Interest if you own the property you live in
  • a Council Tax Reduction
  • a free TV licence if you’re aged 75 or over
  • help with NHS dental treatment, glasses and transport costs for hospital appointments, if you get the Guarantee Credit part of Pension Credit
  • help with your heating costs through the Warm Home Discount Scheme
  • a discount on the Royal Mail redirection service if you’re moving house
